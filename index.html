The Kingdom of Benin

Social Order

Originally, Benin City was an aggregate of small settlements which had allegiance to the king.

Around 1000s, society was hierarchical.

It was headed by a succession of kings, called the Ogisos (sky rulers),

And the king was assisted by the Uzamas (powerful nobles).

Around 1300, the Edo people felt that the ogiso was no longer an effective leader.

Oranmiyan created a new dynasty, calling himself the first Oba of Benin, and so the Obas' regime began.

The Obas would rule Benin for the next six centuries.

Social Organisation

The basic organising principle within both the village and the urban ward is the division of the population into age sets.

Pre Colonial Benin society had a clearly demarcated class structure: a mostly urban elite, comprising the governmental, religious, and educational bureaucracies; a commoner group, consisting of lower-status urbanites, such as artisans; and the peasantry.

Formerly, the king and chiefs had slaves, primarily acquired through warfare, who constituted an agricultural workforce for the elite.

Political Organisation

The Oba's mother was a title holder (iyoba) in one of the palace societies and maintained her own court near Benin City, and his younger brothers were sent to be hereditary chiefs of villages throughout the kingdom, thus constituting part of a limited, rural-based elite.

Besides the king and his family, the political structure consisted of the holders of various chiefly titles, who were organised into three main orders of chiefs: the seven uzama , the palace chiefs, and the town chiefs.

-Each unit of Benin was controlled by a title holder who acted as the intermediary between the villages and the king and whose main duty was to collect taxes and tribute in the form of money (cowries) and goods (cattle, yams etc).

The socio-political organisation exists at two levels; the central and the provincial.

The managerial system in pre-colonial Benin revolved around the oba and his royal court.

From the palace, all directives were issued for onward transmission to all parts of the kingdom.

The Oba is considered as possessing spiritual and mystical powers, which are reinforced annually during the ritual of Igue.

With his enhanced powers, the oba as believed by his subjects is endowed with the capacity to prosper the entire land.

Conflict with the Obas

• Edionisen were the five elders of Benin Kingdom during the reign of Oba Ewedo.

• Oba Ewedo called them "Emwan nei zama omwan" meaning "people who have no respect for their monarch".

• This name was later corrupted to Uzama.


• Ewuare instituted reforms aimed at diminishing the power of the Uzama

• He enacted a rule of primogeniture to eliminate their role in the process of succession to the throne.
